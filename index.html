Math: First Isomorphism Theorem (groups)

Let \\(\\varphi : G \\to H\\) be a group homomorphism with kernel \\(\\ker \\varphi\\). Then the quotient group \\(G / \\ker \\varphi\\) is isomorphic to the image of \\(\\varphi\\):

\ \[ G / \\ker \\varphi \\;\\cong\\; \\operatorname{im}(\\varphi). \\]

Equivalently, there exists a unique isomorphism \\(\\psi : G / \\ker \\varphi \\to \\operatorname{im}(\\varphi)\\) such that \\(\\psi(g \\ker \\varphi) = \\varphi(g)\\) for all \\(g \\in G\\).
