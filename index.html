Math: Normal Sylow subgroup in a group of order 3025

Question: Let \(G\) be a group of order \(3025\). Prove that \(G\) must have at least one normal Sylow subgroup.

Solution:

We have \(|G| = 3025 = 5^2 \cdot 11^2\). Let \(n_{11}\) be the number of Sylow \(11\)-subgroups of \(G\). By Sylow’s theorems, \(n_{11} \mid 5^2 = 25\) and \(n_{11} \equiv 1 \pmod{11}\).

The positive divisors of \(25\) are \(1,5,25\). Checking each modulo \(11\): \(1 \equiv 1 \pmod{11}\), \(5 \equiv 5 \pmod{11}\), \(25 \equiv 3 \pmod{11}\). The only divisor of \(25\) that is congruent to \(1 \pmod{11}\) is \(n_{11} = 1\).

Thus, the Sylow \(11\)-subgroup is unique and hence normal in \(G\). Therefore, \(G\) has at least one normal Sylow subgroup.
